## Overview

This repository accompanies the study presented in **arXiv:26XX.XXXXX**, which investigates whether primordial black holes (PBHs) in the mass range $10^{17},\mathrm{g} \lesssim M_{\rm PBH} \lesssim 10^{28},\mathrm{g}$ can form long-lived density spikes around heavier PBHs with masses of order $M_\odot$ or $10^3 M_\odot$.

For such spikes to survive over cosmological timescales, infalling PBHs must acquire sufficient angular momentum to avoid direct capture by the central black hole. Since PBHs may form with negligible peculiar velocities, additional mechanisms are required to generate the necessary torque.

The focus of this repository is the numerical study of **mutual interactions among light PBHs** in the presence of a fixed central gravitational potential, representing a heavy PBH of mass $M_\odot$. Dedicated GADGET4 simulations are used to quantify the angular momentum growth generated by close gravitational encounters between light PBHs, while analytical estimates provide complementary insight.

This repository is restricted to the study of the light-light interaction channel. Other sources of angular-momentum generation considered in the accompanying paper are not included here, as they are either treated analytically or investigated using the NBodyIMRI code developed by Bradley Kavanagh.

References: <br>
- GADGET-4: Springel et al., Mon. Not. Roy. Astron. Soc. 506 no. 2, (2021) 2871–2949, https://wwwmpa.mpa-garching.mpg.de/gadget4/
-  NBodyIMRI: Kavanagh et al., Phys.Rev.D 111 (2025) 6, 063071 and Phys.Rev.D 111 (2025) 6, 063070, https://github.com/bradkav/NbodyIMRI

## Repository Structure

### `gadget_fixed_potential`

A customized version of GADGET4 for simulating light-PBH dynamics in the gravitational field of a fixed central black hole. The code is optimized to study close encounters and angular momentum generation..
